Football Court

Explore the Excitement of Football

Football, also known as soccer in some regions, is one of the most popular sports globally. Played between two teams of 11 players each, the aim is to score goals by getting the ball into the opponent's net. Football is known for its thrilling pace, skillful plays, and passionate fans. Played both professionally and casually, it is a sport that unites people from all walks of life.

Game Facts and History
  • The origins of modern football can be traced back to 1863 when the Football Association was formed in England.
  • Football is the world's most popular sport, with over 4 billion fans worldwide.
  • The first FIFA World Cup was held in 1930 in Uruguay, and it has been played every four years since then.
  • The sport has evolved into a professional, global industry with clubs and international teams competing at the highest levels.
  • The rules of the game, including the size of the field, the number of players, and the use of a round ball, have remained largely unchanged for over a century.
Court Measurements
  • The standard football field length is between 100 meters to 110 meters.
  • The width of the field should be between 64 meters to 75 meters.
  • The goal area, or the "six-yard box," is 5.5 meters from the goal line and 18.5 meters wide.
  • The penalty area extends 16.5 meters from the goal line and is 40 meters wide.
  • The center circle has a radius of 9.15 meters.
Net Specifications
  • The football goal posts are 7.32 meters wide and 2.44 meters high.
  • The goal net is typically made of durable, strong material like nylon or polypropylene.
  • The net must be securely fastened to the goalposts to prevent the ball from passing through the back of the goal.
  • The net is designed to withstand the force of high-speed balls, ensuring safety and durability during play.
Disclaimer: The specifications listed above are based on the standard regulations used in FIFA competitions. Local variations may exist depending on the level of play or the type of event.
