Volleyball Court

Get Ready to Play the Exciting Game of Volleyball

Volleyball is a fast-paced and exciting team sport played both indoors and outdoors. The goal is simple: send the ball over the net and prevent the opposing team from returning it. Volleyball has evolved into one of the most popular sports worldwide, with international competitions such as the Olympics and World Cup. Whether you play for fun or compete professionally, volleyball is a game that builds teamwork, coordination, and strategy.

Game Facts and History
  • Volleyball was invented by William G. Morgan in 1895 in Holyoke, Massachusetts, USA.
  • The first volleyball game was played with a soccer ball and a badminton net.
  • The sport was initially called "mintonette" before being renamed to "volleyball" in 1896.
  • Volleyball became an official Olympic sport in 1964, with both indoor and beach volleyball events.
  • The game is played by two teams of six players on each side of the net, aiming to score points by sending the ball to the opponent's court.
Court Measurements
  • The court dimensions are 18 meters long and 9 meters wide.
  • The centerline divides the court into two equal halves of 9 meters by 9 meters each.
  • The attack line is positioned 3 meters from the centerline and marks the boundary for front-row players.
  • The service area behind the baseline is 9 meters wide on each side of the court.
Net Specifications
  • The net height for men's volleyball is 2.43 meters, and for women's volleyball, it is 2.24 meters.
  • The net's width is 9.5 meters to cover the full width of the court.
  • The net is made of strong, durable nylon or similar materials to withstand continuous play.
  • The net should be securely fastened and taut to ensure smooth gameplay.
Disclaimer: The dimensions and specifications listed above follow standard regulations for professional volleyball. Local variations may exist depending on the type of court and recreational setup.
